Halfeti, located in the western part of Şanlıurfa, resembles a fairy-tale land with its tranquility and natural beauty. I recently had the opportunity to visit this unique town, and I'm excited to share my experiences with you as if I were writing a blog post.

As soon as I set foot in Halfeti, I was captivated by the peaceful atmosphere surrounding me. However, beneath this tranquility lies a poignant tale—the town's submerged half, inundated by the waters of the Birecik Dam constructed in 2000. Nevertheless, even this story couldn't diminish Halfeti's allure; ancient stone houses, mosques, and trees rising from the water offered me a glimpse of timelessness.

Halfeti's history dates back centuries, bearing the traces of ancient civilizations such as the Assyrian, Hittite, Persian, Macedonian, and Seleucid empires. As I wandered through its streets, I could feel the breath of the past at every turn. The richness of Halfeti's history also reflects the influences of Abbasid, Umayyad, Seljuk, Zengi, and Ayyubid cultures, adding depth to its charm.

One of the things that make Halfeti even more special is its designation as a "Slow City" by the Cittaslow International Coordination Committee in 2013. This highlights the town's commitment to preserving its serene and sustainable way of life. Additionally, recent developments in tourism have brought Halfeti into the spotlight, attracting more visitors each year, especially during the autumn months when its natural beauty is at its peak.

Halfeti is easily accessible, with a population of 40,000 people and located about 110 kilometers from Şanlıurfa. If you choose to visit, you can also explore the historical sites in Şanlıurfa, such as Balıklıgöl (the Pool of Abraham), the Şanlıurfa Archaeology Museum, Urfa Castle, and the extraordinary archaeological site of Göbeklitepe, located just 20 kilometers from the city center.

I wanted to share my experience of Halfeti because of my fascination with this unique town. I hope you get the chance to visit and witness firsthand what I experienced. It's truly an unforgettable journey!
